The problem

It's not your k. It's the comparison.

Standard KNN compares the nearest minority distance against the nearest majority distance. Under unequal sampling, this comparison is biased by construction — no choice of k can undo it.

The structural bias

Under a homogeneous Poisson sampling model, the expected distance to the k-th nearest neighbour of a class scales with that class's local density. Equal densities, unequal sample sizes → the rarer class always sits farther away — purely by sampling.

Poisson scaling law
E[dkc] ∝ (k / λc)1/d
At rank 1: ratio = r1/d, where r = Nmaj/Nmin

Two clouds, same underlying density. The majority just has more points — so it always sits closer to any query, regardless of k.

The insight

The math cancels the dimension.

Equate the expected rank-1 minority distance with the expected rank-keff majority distance. Solve. Dimension d falls out. What remains is closed-form and parameter-free.

1
Poisson scaling
E[dkc] ∝ (k / λc)1/d
Standard result for k-th order statistic of a Poisson process.
2
Equate expectations
E[dkeffmaj] = E[d1min]
Find the majority rank that produces a fair rank-1 comparison.
3
Dimension cancels
keff = r
No hyperparameter, no dimension estimate. Just r = Nmaj/Nmin.

Instead of d1min with d1maj — accounts for sampling-induced density imbalance.

The correction strength

α dampens the correction when minorities are clustered — the most common violation of the Poisson assumption. α = 1 applies full correction; α = 0 recovers standard KNN.

One vote is noisy. Many votes converge.

Multi-vote

Each pair of lines is one vote: votei = [ dimin < di·keffmaj ].

Aggregate evidence.

A rank-1 comparison is fragile. Stacking n_votes independent rank-corrected comparisons reduces variance — and the final decision becomes a vote fraction, naturally giving a probability score.

The family

Five variants. One mechanism.

Each one explores a different bias–variance trade-off on top of the same Poisson-derived core.

KNNFairRank
Core — fixed keff = r, closed-form. The theoretical baseline.
KNNFairRankCV
Tunes α in keff = rα via inner CV. Handles cluster violations.
KNNFairRankJointCV ★
Joint (α, n_votes) search. Champion of threshold metrics — G-Mean, MCC.
KNNFairRankEnsemble
Averages votes over full grid. Smooth scores → ranking metric winner.
KNNFairRankJackknife
LOO over minority ranks. Variance reduction for small Nmin.

Our contributions

Four things that make this our work.

We did not just implement KNN variants for class imbalance. We derived an original correction from first principles, validated it at scale, and produced the theory-data link that closes the loop.

01

Original correction, derived not heuristic

keff = r emerges from Poisson order statistics in three lines — no free parameters, no dimension estimate. Closed-form and parameter-free.

02

Rigorous experimental validation

49 datasets × 50 splits × 10 algorithms. The full Demsar 2006 protocol with Friedman, Wilcoxon-Holm, Cohen’s d and bootstrap CIs.

03

Statistically significant gains

Cohen’s d = +0.88 vs KNNOptK (large effect). Beats SMOTE+KNN on ROC-AUC with Holm-corrected p < 10−4. Universal: 85% win rate.

04

Theory ↔ data link

Spearman ρ = +0.77 between borderline_fraction and our G-Mean gain — FairRank helps exactly where the structural bias is strongest.

Decisions we owned

Three calls that shaped the work.

A

No sklearn KNN in the core loop

The course constraint required no scikit-learn for the KNN itself. We adapted the loop from rushter/MLAlgorithms, kept it pure NumPy/SciPy, then added a vectorised scipy.cdist path that is bit-for-bit identical but much faster.

B

Screen 27 candidates before benchmarking 10

Running a 1-rep screening on 27 algorithms first let us identify four conceptual families that were dominated or redundant, keeping the final benchmark scientifically focused at 10 algorithms. Budget saved: ~6× the compute time.

C

Separate the 9 degenerate datasets

Datasets with Nmin < 20 break stratified 10-fold CV (test folds with zero positives → G-Mean undefined). We moved them to a descriptive annex so they don’t pollute the main statistical battery.

Methodology

Built to survive scrutiny.

Every number on this page was generated from a single canonical CSV (benchmark_5rep.csv, 24,500 rows) with a fully seeded pipeline.

Datasets
49 binary imbalanced datasets · 40 non-degenerate in the main analysis · 9 degenerate (Nmin < 20) separated to a descriptive-only annex
Cross-validation
5 repetitions × 10-fold StratifiedKFold = 50 evaluation splits per pair · 3-fold inner CV for (α, n_votes) tuning · parallelised over 8 cores
Metrics
Threshold: G-Mean, MCC, F1 · Ranking: ROC-AUC, PR-AUC · computed from raw confusion-matrix counts (extensible without re-running)
Hyperparameters
α ∈ {0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00} · n_votes ∈ {1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 10}
Statistical battery
Demšar 2006 protocol: Friedman global → Wilcoxon signed-rank (Holm) → Cohen's d paired → Nemenyi critical difference
Reproducibility
Single random seed 42 in config/settings.yaml · all results from results/tables/benchmark_5rep.csv
